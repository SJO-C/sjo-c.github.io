As of Mid-March, I have been working at an Independent School in Sheffield.  The area I am working in is the IT department, helping keep the school running.

As the school is part of a larger Independent School Trust (similar to a Standard Academy Trust), there are a variety of policies and procedures to be adhered to, as well as a very structured manner of dealing with problems.  The school is a mixed house with Teaching Staff and Students being wholly using Google Chromebooks and associated systems, whilst support staff (such as myself) use Windows 11 PCs. However, the Trust is a Microsoft House through and through, meaning most config is conducted on Windows Server (Active Directory etc.) and then synchronised to Google Cloud.  A side effect of this, unfortunately, is that configuration changes take around 30 minutes to an hour to propagate to the systems being configured, making the processes a bit slower than would be ideal.

This is my first full-time job out of the University of Lincoln.  And as such, I am still finding my feet so to speak.
